# Post-shift beta feedback

Short prompt the founder asks both sides after each beta shift. This is product validation, not feature discovery. Answers feed the trust log, not a backlog of new features.

## Manager prompt (after shift completion)

Send this as a plain message — email, voice note, or in person. Do not build it into the product.

> Quick favour for the beta:
>
> 1. Did the shift post give the marshals enough information to act on?
> 2. Did the applicants you got give you enough signal to choose?
> 3. Did acceptance and contact release make sense in the moment?
> 4. Was any status (open, filled, completed, closed) confusing?
> 5. What felt slower than WhatsApp?
> 6. What felt clearer than WhatsApp?
> 7. Would you use it for the next shift?

## Marshal prompt (after shift completion)

> Quick favour for the beta:
>
> 1. Was the shift post clear enough to apply confidently?
> 2. Was the application flow easy?
> 3. Once accepted, was contact release clear and well-timed?
> 4. Was any status (applied, accepted, rejected, withdrawn) confusing?
> 5. What felt slower than WhatsApp?
> 6. What felt clearer than WhatsApp?
> 7. Would you apply again?

## Classifying answers

Every answer goes into one of four buckets. Do not auto-convert any of them into product work.

- **beta blocker** — the answer reveals the trusted loop is broken (e.g. "I didn't realise I'd been accepted" or "I shared my number in the cover note because I didn't know I shouldn't"). Pause invites; log in [docs/beta-trust-incident-log.md](beta-trust-incident-log.md); repair only the failed gate.
- **hardening item** — small operational fix (e.g. confirmation email wording, a status label). Add to [docs/hardening-backlog.md](hardening-backlog.md).
- **later improvement** — speculative or out-of-scope feature ask (e.g. messaging, ratings, photo upload). Park it. Do not promote to scope.
- **noise** — one-off comment, preference, or off-topic remark. No action.

## What feedback is NOT for

- Not for justifying new features mid-beta.
- Not for adding "just one more thing" to the MVP.
- Not for testimonials or marketing copy.
- Not for public ratings.

The whole point of this beta is to confirm the existing loop holds in real life. Feedback is the lens, not a wishlist.
