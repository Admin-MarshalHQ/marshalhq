# First trusted invite batch

Founder-controlled, manual, narrow. Not automated. Not a public launch.

## Recommended composition

- **1 manager** to start, 2 if there are two real upcoming shifts in the same week.
- **3–8 marshals** total, weighted toward marshals the founder personally trusts to be patient with first-week friction.
- **At least one real upcoming shift** the manager would have posted anyway — a manufactured shift wastes the cycle.
- **Founder available** to watch the entire loop in real time for the first booked shift.

## Hard rules

- No public signup link in any invite copy.
- No mass invites (no Mailchimp, no batched send via the product).
- No onboarding campaigns, no scheduled drip emails, no marketing copy.
- No "share with your network" framing — invitees should know the founder is watching.
- Each invite is sent personally by the founder (email or message) with the live URL, the founder's direct contact, and a short note on what to expect.

## Suggested invite copy (founder-personal, not marketing)

> Hi [name],
>
> MarshalHQ is in a small private beta — I'm watching every shift in real time. The product is narrow on purpose: a manager posts a structured shift, marshals create profiles and apply, the manager picks one, and contact details are released only after that pick.
>
> If you'd like to try it for [specific upcoming shift / for general profile-building], the URL is [LIVE URL]. Sign up with the email I've sent this to. Reply to me directly if anything is unclear or breaks.
>
> Thanks for being one of the first to try it.

## What pauses further invites

Add an invite only after the previous shift has hit at least Gate 7 (contact released cleanly) on [docs/founder-first-shift-checklist.md](founder-first-shift-checklist.md). If any shift fails a gate, no new invites until the gate is repaired. See [docs/beta-trust-incident-log.md](beta-trust-incident-log.md) for the pause/resume log.

## When to stop being founder-personal

Not yet. The whole point of this beta is to keep it founder-controlled. Broad invite mechanics belong to a much later phase, not here.
